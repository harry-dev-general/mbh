Deck Equipment

Life Jackets

Location: Forward storage compartment

Requirements:

  • One jacket per person on board
  • Appropriate sizes for all passengers
  • Children must have properly fitted child-size jackets
  • Check condition before each trip

Inspection Points: Straps, buckles, flotation material, reflective tape

Fire Extinguisher

Location: Near console, easily accessible

Type: Marine-rated dry chemical

Check:

  • Pressure gauge in green zone
  • Pin and seal intact
  • No visible damage
  • Within expiry date

Operation: Pull pin, Aim at base, Squeeze handle, Sweep side to side

First Aid Kit

Location: Waterproof compartment near console

Contents Include:

  • Bandages and gauze
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Emergency blanket
  • CPR mask

Check: Seal intact, contents complete, items within expiry

Navigation Lights

Required Lights:

  • Port (red) - Left side
  • Starboard (green) - Right side
  • Stern (white) - Rear
  • All-round white (when anchored)

Test: Check all lights function before departure

When Required: Between sunset and sunrise, or restricted visibility

Sound Signals

Type: Air horn or electric horn

Required Signals:

  • One short blast - Altering course to starboard
  • Two short blasts - Altering course to port
  • Three short blasts - Operating in reverse
  • Five short blasts - Danger signal

Test: Ensure horn produces clear, loud sound

Outboard Engine

Pre-Start Checks:

  • Oil level - Check dipstick
  • Tell-tale water flow - Confirms cooling
  • Propeller - No damage or debris
  • Tilt/trim operation
  • Emergency stop lanyard attached

Starting Procedure:

  1. Ensure in neutral
  2. Prime if required
  3. Turn key or pull cord
  4. Check water flow from tell-tale

Fuel System

Fuel Type: Unleaded petrol (check specific engine requirements)

Checks:

  • Fuel level - Visual or gauge check
  • Fuel lines - No cracks or leaks
  • Fuel filter - Clean and clear
  • Vent - Open for proper flow
  • Shut-off valve - Know location

Safety: No smoking, check for fumes before starting

Anchor & Rode

Type: Appropriate for vessel size and bottom conditions

Rode Requirements:

  • Length: Minimum 5:1 scope ratio
  • Condition: No fraying or damage
  • Properly secured to vessel
  • Chain leader for better holding

Storage: Secure to prevent movement, ready for quick deployment

Bilge Pump

Types: Manual and/or electric

Operation:

  • Auto mode - Activates with water presence
  • Manual mode - Operator controlled
  • Test both modes before departure

Maintenance: Keep intake clear, test float switch, check discharge
